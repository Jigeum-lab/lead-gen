## The Origins of Entity SEO: Is Google Moving Beyond Keywords?

Since Google's Hummingbird update (2013), Google has evolved away from simply counting keyword frequency toward understanding the meaning and context behind those keywords.

For example, when a user searches for "LeadGenLab," Google tries to determine at the entity level whether LeadGenLab is a content company, an AI marketing agency, or some other company with a similar name. Entity SEO is the work of clearly defining your brand within this framework.

## Entity SEO vs. Core SEO: What's the Difference?

While core SEO focuses on technical factors such as keyword density, backlinks, and page speed, Entity SEO operates at a higher level—telling Google "what our brand is and what relationships it has with which entities." In the era of AI search, Entity SEO is becoming an even more important foundation for brand visibility.

## What Is an Entity? The Unit Through Which AI and Search See the World

## The 3 Types of Entities

Person — Real individuals such as founders, CEOs, and experts. These connect to content author information.

Place — Physical location information such as cities, countries, and company addresses. These connect to your Google Business Profile.

Concept — Abstract ideas such as GEO, AEO, and AI commerce. The strategy of turning AI-era coinages into entities—as LeadGenLab does—is especially important.

## Practical Methods to Strengthen Entity SEO

## 4 Ways to Register Your Brand in the Knowledge Graph

First, align your About page with your Organization schema. Keep your company name, logo, founding date, service type, and social media account information consistent.

Second, secure authoritative external citations, such as Wikipedia. The more your brand appears on authoritative external sites that cover entities, the higher your entity recognition becomes in the knowledge graph.

Third, use your brand name consistently across your content. When various versions of your brand name (abbreviations, misspellings, English forms, etc.) are mixed together, entity recognition declines.

Fourth, keep your Google Business Profile up to date. For local businesses, this connects directly to place entity recognition.

## Connecting Entity SEO with GEO and AEO: Getting AI to Describe Your Brand on Its Own

## The AI Search Impact of a Brand with an Established Entity

When you search a brand name directly in ChatGPT or Perplexity and it returns a substantial amount of information, that is evidence of strong Entity SEO. For brands without an entity, AI may struggle to mention them in answers, or may describe them with inaccurate information.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Q. Is Entity SEO a strategy that only applies to large enterprises?

No. It's needed by startups and small brands as well. In particular, the earlier a brand is being discovered for the first time in AI search, the more important it is to set up Entity SEO early.

## Q. What changes appear in AI search once an entity is established?

When AI searches your brand name, it can easily describe your core information on its own (what kind of company you are, what field you're an expert in). It can mention your brand in answers more frequently and more accurately.

## Q. Does schema markup help with Entity SEO?

Yes, it helps a great deal. Organization, Person, and Product schema are the most direct ways to declare your brand's entity at the data level.

## Q. How are entities different from keywords?

A keyword is the text the user types itself. An entity is the actual object (the real thing) that the keyword refers to. Google understands content around entities rather than keywords.

## Q. Can a newly created brand do Entity SEO?

Even without accumulated entities, you should start by exposing structured data and consistent brand information externally. Over time, a meaningful entity takes shape.

## Q. Do Korean brands need to do Entity SEO on Naver too?

Yes. Naver also performs its own entity-based indexing. Maintaining a consistent brand entity across the Naver homepage, Naver Post, Naver Blog, and other channels is central to Entity SEO in the Korean market.
