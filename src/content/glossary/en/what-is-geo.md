## Why GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Emerged

Search has changed.

Just a few years ago, people typed keywords into Google and clicked a link from the list of results. For a brand to gain visibility, it simply needed to rank on the first page of search results. SEO handled that job.

Today, it's different. Ask ChatGPT to "recommend a telemedicine app," and the AI synthesizes multiple sources and generates an answer directly. Users don't click links. Whether your brand name appears inside that answer—or doesn't—is the new competitive battleground.

GEO is the strategy of designing content for this new structure.

## A Precise Definition of GEO: How It Differs from SEO

## SEO vs. GEO — The Difference in What You Optimize For

SEO optimizes for the ranking algorithms of traditional search engines like Google and Naver. The goal is to rank highly in search results, and the performance metrics are click-through counts and organic traffic.

GEO optimizes for the answer-generation mechanisms of generative AI such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. The goal is for your brand and content to be cited within AI-generated answers, and the performance metrics are AI referral traffic and the frequency of brand mentions within AI search.

The two strategies are not mutually exclusive. The most effective approach today is an integrated strategy that builds GEO on top of an SEO foundation.

## What You Need to Change in Your Content for GEO

## 5 Characteristics of Content That AI Cites

The content AI draws on to generate answers shares a common set of patterns.

First, it has clear definitions and structure. AI favors content with explicit definitions—such as "X is …"—over ambiguous phrasing.

Second, it sends strong E-E-A-T signals. Experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness must be demonstrated directly within the content.

Third, it applies structured data (schema markup). FAQ, HowTo, and Article schema help AI understand the content.

Fourth, it includes specific data and sources. Content backed by statistics, figures, and references is trusted more by AI.

Fifth, it is formatted to answer natural-language questions directly. Content should be structured around the questions users would actually pose to an AI.

## How to Apply GEO Strategy in Practice

## Designing Content Structure for GEO

GEO-optimized content flows in the order of "question → direct answer → context → data → how to act." The first principle is to place the core definition at the very top of the page, with no long introduction.

## Technical Optimization for GEO

You need to check your robots.txt settings so that AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and others) can crawl your site properly. Applying schema markup, optimizing for mobile, and improving page load speed should be carried out alongside this.

## GEO in the Korean Market: Global Platforms + Domestic AI Platforms

In Korea, a GEO strategy must account not only for ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews, but also for Naver AI Search (CUE:), Clova X, and Kakao AI. This is territory that global GEO agencies don't cover—and it represents a differentiation opportunity for domestic brands.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. Is GEO a strategy that replaces SEO?

No. GEO is not a replacement for SEO but an extension of it. The recommended approach is an integrated one: manage your visibility in Google search results with SEO, while additionally securing citations within AI answers through GEO.

Q. How do you measure the impact of GEO?

You measure it through AI referral traffic in Google Analytics (from ChatGPT, Perplexity, and others), your share of AI Overviews among SERP features, and the frequency of brand mentions within AI search.

Q. What should you do first to get started with GEO?

The most efficient sequence is: check whether your current site allows AI crawler access → apply schema markup to key pages → create clear, definitional content for your core keywords.
