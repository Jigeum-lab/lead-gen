## What Is Search Intent: The Real Goal Behind the Keyword

Two people can search the same term, "telemedicine," for entirely different reasons. One wants to learn how to use it, another wants to compare which app is best, and a third is ready to sign up right now. These are three completely different search intents.

Content that fails to grasp search intent won't rank, no matter how many keywords it packs in. That's because both Google and AI evaluate whether content matches the user's search intent.

## The Four Types of Search Intent: Informational, Navigational, Transactional, and Commercial

## Classifying Search Intent for SEO and GEO Strategy

Informational — Intent to obtain information, as in "what is GEO" or "how to use telemedicine." Blog posts, glossaries, and guide content are a good fit. This is the primary target for AEO and GEO optimization.

Navigational — Intent to reach a specific site or page, as in "Dr. Now login" or "LeadGenLab blog." Brand SEO is the key here.

Transactional — Intent to take immediate action, as in "download a telemedicine app" or "request GEO consulting." Landing pages and service pages with clear CTAs are a good fit.

Commercial Investigation — Intent to compare and research before buying or deciding, as in "telemedicine app comparison" or "recommended GEO agencies." Comparison content and case studies are effective.

## How to Analyze Search Intent: Read the SERP First

## A Three-Step Process for Identifying Search Intent

Step 1: Run a direct search for your target keyword.

Review the content types (blog, product page, video, etc.) of the top 5–10 SERP results. The content types that rank highest reflect the dominant search intent for that keyword.

Step 2: Analyze the content format.

If the top results are all list-style, go with a list format; if they're guide-style, match that with a guide format.

Step 3: Check People Also Ask.

The related questions that appear in PAA reveal the more granular search intents tied to that keyword.

## Why Search Intent Matters Even More in AI Search

## How AI Answer Generation Relates to Search Intent

AI doesn't simply match keywords—it grasps the user's intent to generate an answer. The more precisely your content aligns with search intent, the more likely AI is to treat it as a trustworthy source and cite it.

AI citation frequency is highest for informational queries in particular. That's exactly why informational content is the primary target of GEO and AEO strategy.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Q. What happens if I misread search intent?

Content that doesn't match search intent struggles to rank, even when it's loaded with keywords. For example, building a service sales page for the keyword "what is GEO" fails to satisfy informational intent, so it's bound to rank low.

## Q. What if a single keyword carries multiple search intents?

For mixed-intent keywords, analyze the top SERP results to identify the dominant intent and prioritize the matching content type. From there, an effective approach is to add sections that cover the secondary intents.

## Q. Can you sum up the relationship between search intent and content type in one line?

Informational → blogs and guides; navigational → homepages and brand pages; transactional → landing pages and product pages; commercial investigation → comparison content and case studies.

## Q. Isn't keyword analysis alone enough, without analyzing search intent?

Keyword analysis tells you what to write; search intent analysis tells you how to write it. You need both. Alignment with search intent is a core evaluation criterion for both SEO rankings and AI citations.

## Q. Does search intent matter more in AI search than in traditional SEO?

It matters equally. That said, AI also handles conversational queries with more complex, compound intent. Because AI search interprets not just a single intent but the surrounding context and background to formulate answers, the depth and contextual understanding of your content become even more important.

## Q. Do Naver and Google classify search intent differently?

The basic classification is similar, but Naver tends to show stronger commercial-investigation intent in shopping and local search. For Naver SEO, we recommend analyzing the Naver SERP separately.
