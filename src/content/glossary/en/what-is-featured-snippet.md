## What Is a Featured Snippet: Why It's Called Position Zero

Google search rankings start at position one. A featured snippet, however, appears above the number-one result. That's where the name "Position Zero" comes from.

When you earn this spot, your brand's content is shown before the user clicks any link. It occupies a position even higher than the top organic result.

## Types of Featured Snippets: How They Appear

## The 4 Types of Featured Snippets

Paragraph — the most common format. It summarizes the answer to a question in two to three sentences. It frequently appears for definitional and explanatory queries such as "What is X" or "Why does X matter."

List — displays either ordered steps (1, 2, 3…) or an unordered list of items. It commonly appears for queries like "How to do X" or "Types of X."

Table — presents comparisons or numeric data in table form. It frequently appears for "A vs. B comparison" queries.

Video — surfaces a specific segment of a YouTube video as the answer.

## How to Optimize Content to Win Featured Snippets

## 5 Principles for Featured Snippet Optimization

Principle 1: Use the question directly in your H2 and H3 headings. Google looks for the heading that best matches the user's query. Write the exact phrasing a user would type as your heading, such as "What is a featured snippet?"

Principle 2: Place a direct answer of 50–100 characters immediately below the heading. Google most often draws featured snippet candidates from the first paragraph beneath a heading.

Principle 3: For paragraph snippets, two to three sentences are optimal; for list snippets, six to eight items. If the content is too long, it gets truncated in the display.

Principle 4: Target keywords that already have a featured snippet. Capturing the snippet from a keyword that already shows one is a more efficient strategy than trying to create a snippet for a keyword that has none.

Principle 5: Add an FAQ section. Structuring related questions as an FAQ lets a single page capture multiple snippets at once.

## How Featured Snippets Connect to AEO and GEO

Featured snippets are the most direct target of AEO.

Winning a snippet increases the likelihood that the same answer will also be cited in Google's AI Overviews, so you target AEO and GEO simultaneously.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. If I win a featured snippet, do I lose my number-one position?

Google sometimes excludes a page that has won a featured snippet from the regular organic results. You may lose the number-one position, but because the snippet appears in a higher spot, total clicks are often maintained or even increase.

Q. Does a featured snippet appear for every search query?

No. Snippets mainly appear for queries with clear informational intent (definitions, how-tos, comparisons). They rarely appear for transactional queries (purchases, bookings).

Q. How can I check whether I'm showing in a featured snippet?

It's difficult to confirm directly in Google Search Console. You can check featured snippet ownership using the "SERP Features" filter in SEO tools such as Ahrefs or SEMrush.
