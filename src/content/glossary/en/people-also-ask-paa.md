## What Is People Also Ask (PAA): Defining Google's Related Questions Section

When you search for something on Google, a box of "People Also Ask" questions appears in the middle of the results. This is People Also Ask (PAA).

Click a single question and the answer expands, while new related questions are added at the same time. The more a user explores, the more the list of questions keeps growing. Because users can get answers to multiple questions without leaving the results page, PAA is one of the factors accelerating the zero-click phenomenon.

## Why PAA Matters for SEO, GEO, and AEO Strategy

## 3 Ways Owning PAA Benefits Your Brand

PAA is not just another search result element. It connects directly to brand visibility and AI citations.

First, it becomes a source for Google AI Overviews. Google AI Overviews tend to use content frequently cited in PAA as a source. Content that appears in PAA is therefore more likely to be cited in AI Overviews as well.

Second, it completes your Position Zero strategy alongside the Featured Snippet. If the Featured Snippet is the top answer to a specific question, PAA increases your brand's share of the results page by covering the full set of related questions.

Third, it captures long-tail keyword traffic. The questions that appear in PAA reflect what users genuinely want to know next. Including these questions in your content drives traffic from a variety of long-tail keywords beyond your primary keyword.

## Content Characteristics That Earn Google PAA Visibility

## Content Patterns Google Selects as PAA Answers

The content Google selects as PAA answers shares common patterns.

It puts the question directly in the heading. Sentences a user would actually type—such as "What is People Also Ask?" or "How do I get featured in PAA?"—are used as H2s and H3s.

A direct answer of 40 to 60 characters comes right below the heading. PAA boxes do not accommodate long paragraphs. A structure that states the key point first and adds detailed explanation afterward works well.

FAQPage Schema is applied. Marking up your FAQ with structured data makes it easier for Google to recognize that Q&A as a PAA candidate.

## A Content Writing Strategy for PAA Optimization

## 4 Practical Principles for Entering PAA

Principle 1: Start by collecting the questions currently shown in PAA. Search Google for your target keyword and review the PAA question list. These questions become the list of H3s you need to include in your content.

Principle 2: Write one clear answer per question. Pairing one H3 with one clear answer is the foundation of PAA optimization. Do not combine two or more questions into a single section.

Principle 3: Always apply FAQPage Schema. Using the FAQ block in WordPress plugins like Rank Math or Yoast SEO generates the schema automatically. You can also write the JSON-LD directly.

Principle 4: Reference the answer length and format of existing PAA holders. Check which sites currently own the PAA in Ahrefs or SEMrush, and use the structure and length of their answers as a benchmark for your content.

## How PAA Connects to AI Overviews

## How PAA Optimization Impacts GEO and AEO

PAA and AI Overviews may look like separate areas, but they are deeply connected. When Google AI Overviews generate an answer, they tend to give priority to content frequently cited in PAA. This is why PAA optimization becomes part of a GEO strategy.

In addition, external AI search engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity also learn the question patterns that frequently appear in Google PAA. PAA-optimized content structured as natural-language questions is more likely to be cited in external AI search as well.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Q. How many People Also Ask questions are displayed?

Four questions are displayed at first. Each time you click a question, a new one is added, so the list can theoretically expand infinitely.

## Q. Does appearing in PAA increase click-through rate?

PAA itself is a zero-click driver. Because the answer expands in place, clicks often do not occur. That said, some users do click the answer's source link, so it generates some traffic—and its value lies more in brand visibility and credibility.

## Q. What is the difference between PAA and a Featured Snippet?

A Featured Snippet appears at the very top of the results as a single answer box. PAA appears in the middle of the results as a list of multiple questions, each of which can have its own independent answer source. It is even possible for a single page to own both a Featured Snippet and PAA at the same time.

## Q. How long does it take to appear in PAA?

It varies with content quality and site authority, but new content with FAQPage Schema applied takes an average of 2 to 8 weeks to enter PAA.

## Q. Does PAA appear for every search query?

No. It appears mainly for queries with informational intent (definitions, methods, comparisons, reasons). PAA often does not appear for branded searches or transactional-intent queries.

## Q. How do I check whether I own PAA?

You can review the PAA ownership status for a specific keyword in Ahrefs' SERP Overview or SEMrush's SERP Features filter. It is difficult to check PAA visibility directly in Google Search Console.

## Q. Can I get into PAA without FAQPage Schema?

Yes. Even without schema, content can enter PAA when its structure is clear. However, applying FAQPage Schema makes it easier for Google to recognize the Q&A structure, which increases your chances of entering.
