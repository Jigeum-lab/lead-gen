## Why SERP Features Matter: The Search Results Page Has Changed

Search for something on Google right now. You'll almost never see a result page with just ten blue links.

AI Overviews, related questions, image packs, knowledge panels, reviews, videos — SERP Features now occupy more than half the screen. In many cases, the standard organic results (blue links) don't even appear until you scroll down.

This shift points to one conclusion: managing keyword rankings alone is no longer enough. Which SERP Features you appear in has become the core of search visibility.

## The Main Types of SERP Features and Their Characteristics

## The Key SERP Features in the Age of AI Search

AI Overview — Introduced in 2024. Displays an AI-generated, comprehensive answer at the very top of the search results. A primary target for GEO and AEO strategies.

Featured Snippet — A direct answer presented in a box. Often called Position Zero. It can appear as a paragraph, a list, or a table.

People Also Ask (PAA) — A list of related questions. Clicking one expands the answer right in place. You can capture these through an AEO strategy.

Knowledge Panel — Displays summary information about a company, person, or place in a card on the right-hand side. Connected to Google Business Profile.

Image Pack — Displays related images arranged horizontally. You can enter it by optimizing your visual content.

Shopping Results — Displays product images, prices, and retailers. Connected to Google Merchant Center.

Local Pack — In location-based searches, displays three nearby businesses alongside a map. You enter it through local SEO strategy.

Video Results — Displays YouTube videos within the results. You enter it by optimizing video content.

Sitelinks — On brand searches, displays links to key subpages alongside the main result.

## How SERP Features Connect to GEO, AEO, and SEO Strategy

## Strategies for Capturing Each SERP Feature

SERP Features are not displayed at random. You can deliberately enter them through content and technical optimization.

AI Overviews, Featured Snippets, and People Also Ask are won through AEO and GEO strategies. Question-style headings, clear definition boxes, FAQPage Schema, and strengthened E-E-A-T are the keys.

Knowledge Panels are entered by strengthening your brand entity within Google. Your official website, Google Business Profile, Wikipedia, and media mentions all serve as signals.

Shopping Results and the Local Pack require managing Google Merchant Center and Google Business Profile, respectively, as prerequisites.

## How to Measure SERP Feature Share

## How to Track SERP Feature Performance

In Google Search Console's Search Performance report, you can review clicks and impressions by search type. With Ahrefs or SEMrush, you can see which SERP Features appear for a given keyword and which of them your site occupies.

When LeadGenLab worked with Drnow, the very first early-performance metric we checked was SERP Feature share. In particular, share of "AI Overview" and "People Also Ask" is the metric that most quickly demonstrates the impact of GEO and AEO work.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. How many SERP Features are there?

There is no number officially defined by Google, but roughly 20 to 30 types are currently identified. Google continuously introduces new formats, so this keeps changing.

Q. Can I target all SERP Features at once?

You should select and pursue the features relevant to your business type. A B2B service would focus on AI Overviews, Featured Snippets, and PAA, while a local business would prioritize the Local Pack.

Q. If I increase my SERP Feature share, will my regular rankings rise too?

There is no direct causal relationship, but the content optimization required to enter SERP Features (clear structure, E-E-A-T, Schema Markup) often has a positive effect on standard organic rankings as well.
