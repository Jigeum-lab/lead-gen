## The Rise of Generative AI Search: Search Has Fundamentally Changed

Before Google existed, people found information by clicking through categories in the Yahoo directory. Then Google arrived and keyword search became the standard. And now, the way we search is changing once again.

Generative AI search has erased the boundary between "search" and "answer." When you enter a question, the AI reads dozens of sources and gives you an answer that it summarizes and synthesizes on the spot. Users no longer need to click through links.

## Traditional Search vs. Generative AI Search: What's Different

## Three Core Differences in How Search Works

A difference in input — Traditional search takes keyword-level input, like "telehealth app." Generative AI search takes conversational, full-sentence input, like "I work a full-time job—can you recommend an app where I can get medical care at home on weekends?"

A difference in result format — Traditional search shows a list of links. The user has to choose and click. Generative AI search produces a single synthesized answer. Getting the information is complete without a click.

A difference in how sources are handled — In traditional search, the source of each link is shown separately. Generative AI search synthesizes multiple sources internally and presents them as a single answer. Sources are either invisible or shown only as footnotes.

## A Comparison of Major Generative AI Search Platforms

## The Characteristics of ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews

ChatGPT (OpenAI) — A conversational interface. It remembers context and supports follow-up questions. Its Search feature crawls the web and then generates an answer. It cites content frequently on recommendation and comparison queries.

Perplexity AI — An AI search engine that cites sources with numbered references. It favors specialized information and data-driven content. B2B and technical content tends to get cited particularly well.

Google AI Overview — An AI-generated answer inserted at the very top of Google search results. Because it is tied to existing Google SEO, SEO work also influences your visibility in AI Overviews.

Naver AI Briefing — Naver's AI search service. It generates answers centered on domestic content, making it one of the most important AI search platforms for Korean brands.

## What Generative AI Search Means for Marketers

## The Structure of the Competition for Brand Visibility Has Changed

In traditional search, it was enough to rank somewhere in the top 1–10 positions. In generative AI search, what matters is whether your brand name appears inside the answer the AI generates. The paradigm has shifted from a competition for rankings to a competition for citations.

In this structure, what marketers need to do is clear: when the AI explains a given topic, you have to make it conclude that "this brand's content is the most trustworthy." That is the purpose of GEO and AEO.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. Will generative AI search completely replace traditional Google search?

A complete replacement in the short term is unlikely. That said, the share of AI search is growing rapidly for certain types of queries (recommendations, comparisons, how-to). For now, the most realistic strategy is to run both channels in parallel.

Q. How do you measure traffic from generative AI search?

In Google Analytics, you filter referral sources such as chatgpt.com, perplexity.ai, and bing.com/chat separately. We recommend setting up an AI referrer segment in GA4.

Q. What are the characteristics of content that gets cited well in generative AI search?

Content with clear definitions, specific data, real-world examples, and expert perspectives. AI prefers concrete, trustworthy information over vague generalities.
