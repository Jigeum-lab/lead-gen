## SEO, GEO, AEO: Why You Need to Distinguish All Three

If you're a marketer, you've probably been asked this at least once: "Isn't GEO just SEO?"

It isn't. The search landscape has split into three tracks, and each requires a different optimization approach. If you fail to distinguish them, you'll either pour all your effort into SEO only to fall behind competitors in AI search, or focus solely on GEO and lose your baseline organic traffic.

## What Is SEO: The Definition and Role of Traditional Search Engine Optimization

## The Core Concept of SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the strategy of optimizing for high rankings on the results pages of traditional search engines such as Google and Naver. Its primary components are keyword optimization, backlink building, technical optimization (site speed, structure), and content quality.

Its performance metrics are organic traffic, keyword rankings, and CTR (click-through rate). Traffic only occurs when a user clicks a link.

## What Is GEO: The Definition and Role of Generative AI Search Optimization

## The Core Concept of GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the strategy of optimizing so that generative AI systems such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews cite your brand content when generating answers. In a model where AI learns from and synthesizes multiple sources to produce an answer, the goal is to become one of those sources.

Its performance metrics are AI referral traffic, the frequency of brand mentions within AI search, and SERP Features share.

## What Is AEO: The Definition and Role of AI Answer Surface Optimization

## The Core Concept of AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is the strategy of optimizing so that your content appears in instant-answer surfaces within Google search results, such as Featured Snippets, People Also Ask, and AI Overviews. A clear question-and-answer structure and FAQPage schema are central to it.

## The Structure of an Integrated SEO, GEO, and AEO Strategy

There's no need to split these three strategies into separate projects. Build one strong piece of content, and you can produce all three effects at once.

Top of the page: A clear definition box → targets AEO (Featured Snippets)

Body structure: Question-style H2s and H3s + direct answers → targets AEO (PAA) + GEO (AI citation)

Content depth: Expert analysis, data, case studies → targets GEO (AI learning source)

Technical optimization: Schema Markup, keyword placement → targets SEO + AEO simultaneously

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. Of the three, which matters most right now?

If you don't have an SEO foundation, start with SEO. If your SEO foundation is reasonably in place, the most effective sequence right now is to capture SERP Features through AEO while simultaneously adding a GEO content strategy.

Q. Do GEO and AEO need to be managed separately?

In practice, it's more efficient to manage them together within a single content strategy. A content structure optimized for AEO (clear definitions, FAQs) also works in your favor for GEO.

Q. How does Naver SEO connect to these three strategies?

Naver operates its own AI search (CUE:) and Clova X, so alongside Naver SEO you should also consider a separate GEO and AEO strategy for Naver's AI platforms.
